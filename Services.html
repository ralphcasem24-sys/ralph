₱10. Lumpiang shanghai

Lumpiang Shanghai (also known as Filipino spring rolls, or simply lumpia or lumpiya) is a Filipino deep-fried appetizer consisting of a mixture of giniling (ground pork) with vegetables like carrots, chopped scallions or red onions and garlic, wrapped in a thin egg crêpe.

₱8. Lumpiang Togue

A crispy Filipino spring roll filled with sautéed mung bean sprouts, carrots, and other fresh vegetables. Each bite offers a satisfying crunch and a burst of savory flavor — best enjoyed with a sweet and spicy vinegar dip. Perfect as a snack or appetizer, Lumpiang Togue brings comfort and tradition to every serving.

₱8. Lumpiang Isda

A flavorful twist on the classic spring roll, Lumpiang Isda is filled with tender flaked fish, mixed vegetables, and savory spices, all wrapped in a golden, crispy wrapper. It’s a delicious and nutritious option that perfectly balances the rich taste of fish with the light crunch of lumpia — best served with a tangy dipping sauce.
